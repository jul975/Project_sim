Do you have a determinism-breaking bug right now?

Not necessarily, if:

you never use master_ss after init,

you never use agent_seed.spawn() after init,

and your current tests (forward clone + full trajectory) are passing.

Do you have a “future gun to the foot”?

Yes, absolutely:

the moment you add new streams via .spawn() on reconstructed SeedSequences (agent_seed, master_ss), your current reconstruction scheme can produce divergence.

schema v1 may not catch environment divergence.

How to make this future-proof without breaking lineage

You need to choose and enforce one of these invariants:

Option 1 — Explicit lineage only (recommended for your plan)

Invariant: After init, you never call .spawn() on SeedSequences that must survive clone/restore.

For new streams (combat/weather), derive them explicitly from stable keys:

e.g., SeedSequence(entropy=agent_entropy, spawn_key=agent_spawn_key + (STREAM_TAG,))

or spawn_key + (STREAM_TAG, stream_index)

Track only your explicit counters (like agent_spawn_count) and include them in snapshot/hashing later.

Treat agent_seed as an init-only splitter. Treat master_ss as init-only.

This matches your stated mental model.

Option 2 — True SeedSequence continuation

If you want to call .spawn() later on agent_seed or master_ss, then you must:

track “children spawned so far” yourself

restore by recreating the SeedSequence and advancing it (spawn(k) and discard)

and include these counters in snapshot

Your current “append spawn_count to spawn_key” is not that.
